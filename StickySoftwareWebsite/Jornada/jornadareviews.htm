Case in point NEW

When I got my new Jornada 420 home, I discovered a booklet in the box from Widget: a company which has been supplying software and peripherals to the PDA market for years. I was delighted to see on the back page that they were advertising a case for the Jornada 820, and as you can probably tell by looking around this site, this is one thing which I've been desperate to get hold of.

The case isn't actually custom designed for the Jornada 820, instead it's a more of a general "mobile communications" case. For this reason, when first opened there is a removable mobile phone carrier and a little bag for storing other executive whatsits. Once these are removed, the Jornada fits snugly into place. You would have a hard job convincing anyone that the case wasn't a specific Jornada option, the fit is that good. (Presumably, smaller handheld PCs can hit inside along with the phone.) Once unzipped, you can open the Jornada screen and as this hinges open, the case opens up too: no need to remove your computer to check an address, or consult the Pocket Streets map.

If you like, you can attach the supplied self-adhesive velco (tm) pads to the bottom of your Jornada to hold it snugly in place, but I'm always wary of sticking anything to my expensive computers and so didn't bother. The reasonably high lip around the bottom of the case stopped the Jornada slipping out, so it wasn't strictly necessary. Nice to have the option though, as the lower half of the case is all "anti-velcro" and will lock the pads into place.

I found that the leather from which the case is made is a little smooth and shiny, which means it could possibly slip off your knees if you were trying to type on a train. Perhaps you would be better putting the velcro pads on the outside of the case, and on your upper-thighs!

As it's a really designed as a general purpose case (let's be honest, it's a hand-bag - aka purse - for men) there are plenty of little pen holders, card holders and zipped pockets. I was able to store some CF cards, pens, and business cards comfortably. Despite being surprisingly thick, it wasn't possible to squeeze in the Jornada PSU unit, so that item would have to travel separately in your main baggage if you needed it with you. Like wise, my USB mouse would have to make its own way.

The overall size (19.5cm x 5cm x 29cm approximately) is a lot less than a notebook case or briefcase, so it's easier to carry around on a plane, or when taking the bus. A little wrist loop can be clipped onto the spine of the case to help hold it, as it is quite heavy when the Jornada is placed inside it.

The top and bottom of the case have strengthened panels, and so I'm quite certain that in the event of dropping it, your Jornada would survive. I'm afraid I wasn't prepared to test this hypothesis with my own Jornada!

Bottom line: Yes, the case is a little pricey. However, it's a well-made peripheral which will provide good protection for your expensive computer, and you can be sure that it will last for a good few year.

The size is a good match for the Jornada, as long as you don't need to carry your mouse/PSU/mains lead with you at all times, and I can't see how it would be possible to make a case which was more suited. Well done. Widget!

Making a good case?

The Pouch place is really just around the corner from me.  I went there today and picked up a case for the Jornada.  I was lucky -- they pretty much make these to order and don't have a lot of stock.

I got a black one, but they have a bunch of bright colors available -- neon hues -- these are used just on the front, so if you purchase one in color, the back, the strap, and the flap that folds over the front are black.

The case fits snugly and the computer slips in easily.  The neoprene fabric seems like it will add a measure of protection, which is the main reason I wanted the case.  The pouch on the back holds the three cables that came with the computer, without much room for anything else.

Really very simple design, just the front compartment that fits the computer exactly and the back pouch for the cables.  Sturdy shoulder strap with sturdy clip-on fasteners -- so you can ditch the strap if you don't need it. Nothing fancy, not beautiful, but seems very servicable and is not expensive. 

Product: The Pouch
Reviewed by: Peggy
Price: $19.95 plus S&H
Available from: http://www.thepouch.com/
