 20th April 1999
Sticky Buttons!
Sticky Software goes colour, with three new
programs for the HP Jornada 420, colour
palmsize PC.

First of all we have the colour version of TankZone,
a 3D real-time shoot-em-up which can be used with
on-screen controls or via the jog wheel and side
buttons.

Then to keep track of your batteries, Palm-power View
adds a new icon to the tooltray and a pop-up graph of
energy usage. This program is free, so try it out.

Finally, for the time being, welcome back an old
favourite. Block is the sliding puzzle of old, this time
with 256 colour pictures to re-arranged. This program
also works on MIPS based colour Palmsize PCs, but
you'll need almost 500K free to run it: might be one for
the CF storage card.

The December 1998 edition of UK magazine PC Plus
has reviewed Pocket Universe for the Handheld PC.
We're glad to say that the review was favourable:

"Overall, we found Pocket Universe extremely easy to
get to grips with. We were able to use it successfully
on a clear night to locate and name a range of
constellations and stars with the naked eye".
"Easy to use, affordable, ultra-portable". "

Speaking of Pocket Universe, a novel use for it has
recently come to our attention. Film makers need
to know where the sun is going to be on certain dates
in the future to help plan their shooting schedules.
Pocket Universe is ideal for this, as it can tell you
the exact location of the sun (and let you see it
on-screen), for any date in the future and for any
location on Earth.

Finally, Pocket Universe users will be glad to know
that a new update is planned for next month, and part
of this will include a Mobile Channel for keeping you
up-to-date. In the meantime, don't forget the Leonids
on the 17th of November!
