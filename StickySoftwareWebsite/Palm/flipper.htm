Introduction

What are the two things Palmsize PC owners want the most from their User Interface?

That's easy:

1. A way to shut down open applications.

Microsoft's guidelines for the Palmsize PC request that developers don't include any options to close currently running applications. The idea is to let Windows CE look after the memory management itself, leaving you to just to get on with using the computer. If you are finished using the Notetaker for example, you just start the next application and Notetake with vanish into the background. If you need it again, you launch it from the Start menu as before: and WindowsCE will move it to the front of the display.

In theory this is a great idea. In practice, many users don't feel right about it. We want to shut down the programs we no longer need. We even go the extent of opening the pop-up keyboard and entering CTRL and Q to do it "manually". We still want the control over our programs which the Handheld PC and desktop versions of Windows give us. We don't want to wait for WindowsCE to run out of memory and shut down the applications: we want to do it ourselves!

2. A way to quickly return to frequently used applications.

As you normally don't or can't shut down applications, the programs in the background are wasted: they sit there sucking up resources. To get to them, you have to open the Start menu and find and launch them again - what a waste of time! How much better if you could just jump to them with only a few taps on the screen. Quicker, easier, more logical and more consistant with the Microsoft ideal of keeping the applications open in the first place.

Features and benefits

Flipper is a small program which achieves both these much-needed tasks in one, unobtrusive taskbar icon. Flipper is this easy to use:

Single-tap on the icon, and the currently open tasks in the background are shuffled around. Your current program (or desktop) flips to the background, and a previous program flips to the foreground. Keep single-tapping and all the open applications will appear, one after the other.

Double-tap on the icon, the currently open task (excluding the desktop) will be closed. No fuss, no hunting for the Task Manager. One click, and the current task is history. This is without a doubt, the easiest way yet to keep your applications and tasks in check.

Tap-and-hold on the icon, and you open a menu. The top items in the menu are the currently running tasks. Select any of them to flip to that task immediately.

That's only half of what FlipperPro can do. From the pop-up menu, you can quickly manage your PsPC. Flip to other tasks, kill all other running programs, open the hidden Windows CE Run... dialog. And we're still not done yet!

Now the fun really starts. Open the Options Dialog, and you'll see a window like this. The colour animated icons help you keep track of what's happening on your system. Worried about power? or memory? Check the current values, and jump straight to the revelant control panel setting windows.

There are still two new features to come:

Autokill. Active this, and your PsPC will look after unused applications running in the background. Don't worry about losing memory, or forgetting to shut down programs. Relax, and let FlipperPro search for unwanted applications every few minutes and close them down. While this method of working mightn't suit everyone, it can be a useful tool.

Startup Sound. What everyone wants! Why not make your PsPC let you know you've switched it on? If you have a CF installed, this option will cause a sound-effect to be triggered when you power up your PDA. Add amusing sound-effects! The option can be turned off when you are running low on battery power, or when you have an alarm set.

Installation and Use

  1. Installing FlipperPro is easy. Download the self-extracting installation file to your desktop PC.
  2. Connect up your Palmsize PC as usual, as though you were performing a standard synchronisation.
  3. Run the downloaded file.
  4. It will extract the software, and launch your Windows CE Services Installation Utility
  5. Follow the instructions, and let FlipperPro install itself in the default directory
  6. Start it up! You'll find it in Start/Programs/Accessories
  7. A new icon will apear in your taskbar: tap one to flip tasks, double-tap to kill the current task, and tap-and-hold to pop open the menu.
  8. Select Options from the menu to configure FlipperPro, or stop it from running.

 

Hints and Tips

Customise the sound!

If you have a CF card on your PsPC, you can make use of the Startup Sound option. This triggers a sound everytime you switch on your PsPC. Don't stick with the supplied sound -- use your imagination. Add your favourite sound effect by copying a standard WAV file to the Windows/ directory on your PsPC.

Keep it alive!

Make FlipperPro restart everytime you perform a warm-restart. Copy the shortcut from the menu to the directory Windows/Startup/ and it will be launched everytime the PsPC reboots.

Button it

Assign FlipperPro to one of the user-definable buttons on the PsPC. When you press the button, Flipper will flip to the next running application automatically.

 

 

 

FAQ

Q. Am I entitled to a free upgrade?

A. If you bought a previous version of FlipperPro, then yes, you are. You should be receiving the upgrade automatically from The Palmsize PC Store.

Q. Can FlipperPro work on a handheld PC?

A. It's not designed for the handheld PC.

Q. I have a new colour Palmsize PC (Wyverne). Will it work?

A. Yes, it's been tested on a Jornada 420. The display has been improved to include colour.

Q. How can I make the menu pop-up?

A. Tap on the flipper icon and hold: previous versions of Flipper required a tap-slide-release motion -- this is no longer used.

Q. I have the free demo. How can I shut it down?

A. Tap on the taskbar near the icon, slide over it, and release.

Q. I can't hear my startup sound!

A. Make sure the option is turned on. This feature only works if a CF (Compact Flash) card is present in your system. If you have copied over your own file, make sure the WAV file is compatible with your PsPC hardware: 8bit, 11kHz is a good minimum. You cannot trigger MP3 files.
