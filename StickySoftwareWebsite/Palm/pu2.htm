  Ever been out at night and wondered what a particular
constellation was called? Or tried to work out where the
planets are in the sky? Or maybe even tried to find the
Andromeda Galaxy?

Maybe you like the warmth of being inside, but would still like to
watch the Moon as it changes phase, or watch as Orion rises and
moves across the sky?

Try Pocket Universe and you'll be amazed at how much your tiny
little handheld computer can do. It's the ideal companion for
amateurs and beginners, and will help you find your way about
the sky.
 

Features

  • Online help
  • Easy to use, standard installation routine
  • Plots the positions of the Sun, Moon and Planets
  • Plots the positions of almost 5000 stars
  • Choose to view only the brighter stars
  • Instant Goto to find a planet or particular location in the sky
  • Works from any position and timezone on Earth
  • EPOCH 2000 star co-ordinates
  • Powerful built-in "time machine"
  • Auto-lock to object: watch as the sun rises and sets
  • Invert the screen colours to make viewing easier
  • Tap the display to instantly goto that location
  • Variable Zoom in and out, with full sky view
  • Many display elements may be toggled on and off
  • Outlines for major constellations, includng the zodiac.
  • Names of brightest stars
  • Sun and Moon rendered at "actual size".
  • Lunar phases
  • Messier objects
  • Location of Jovian moons
  • Save and Load settings, for different locations or preferences
  • Floating toolbar for quickly viewing N,S,E,W and zooms
 



Demo Version
Please feel free download this demonstration version of
Pocket Universe for the palmsized PC. Please note that
this program requires approx 400K of memory free on
the PsPC.

This demo version is not time limited, but has a reduced
number of features. It does not contain a full star catalog,
nor on-line help.
