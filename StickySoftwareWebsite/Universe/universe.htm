 Ever been out at night and wondered what a particular constellation was called? Or tried to work out where the planets are in the sky? Or maybe even tried to find the Andromeda Galaxy?

Maybe you like the warmth of being inside, but would still like to watch the Moon as it changes phase, or watch as Orion rises and moves across the sky?

Good news: one of the most ambitious Windows CE programs yet created makes all this possible. Try Pocket Universe and you'll be amazed at how much your tiny little handheld computer can do. It's the ideal companion for amateurs and beginners, and will help you find your way about the sky.

Features

  • Online help
  • Easy to use, standard installation routine
  • Plots the positions of the Sun, Moon and Planets
  • Support for 480 and 640 pixel screens
  • Plots the positions of almost 5000 stars
  • Not a static map - a unique dynamic view!
  • Choose to view only the brighter stars
  • Instant Goto to find a planet or particular location in the sky
  • Works from any position and timezone on Earth
  • EPOCH 2000 star co-ordinates
  • Powerful built-in "time machine", which can step through seconds, hours, or years
  • Auto-lock to planet: watch as the sun rises and sets during the day
  • Invert the screen colours to make viewing easier
  • Tap the display to instantly goto that location
  • Variable Zoom in and out, with full sky view
  • Many display elements may be toggled on and off (stars, planets, horizon)
  • Outlines for major Northern constellations
  • Names of brightest stars
  • Sun and Moon rendered at "actual size".
  • Lunar phases
  • Messier objects
  • Location of Jovian moons
  • Settings stored in Registry for ease of use
  • Floating toolbar for quickly viewing N,S,E,W and zooms
  • Colour displays on HPCs with colour screens
  • Pretty moon graphics!

  • Installation by NStall
