Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Does Pocket Universe run on the H/PC Pro?

A. If the HPC Pro in question uses the MIPS or SH3 processor, then yes. Pocket Universe should automatically adjust its screen size to suit. For example, users tell us that Pocket Universe runs on the Vadem Clio / Sharp TriPad. Models based on other processers, such as the new HP Jornada based on the StrongARM processor, are not currently supported. When the necessary tools become available (not always easy in Europe, sadly) a compatible version will be released.

Q. Does Pocket Universe support colour displays?

A. Yes, the Handheld PC version will display in colour (blue sky, green glass, red grid lines) on colour models. You can switch to a more contrast-y monochrome view if you wish.

Q. Where is the on-line help in the Palmsize PC version?

A. The Microsoft guidelines explain how all Palmsize PC programs which offer on-line help should do so not with a separate menu (which would take up too much space on the screen) but by using the Help option present in the Start Bar. In other words, all you have to do to read Pocket Universe's on-line help is click on Start and Help when the program is running :-)

Q. Are there any other programs for handheld computers like Pocket Universe?

A. Currently not. There are some other astronomy programs, but they work in a completely different way. Rather than generating a dynamic view for your location, they feature a static star map. It's like holding a piece of paper over your head -- much harder to use than Pocket Universe.

Q. When installing, I get a message telling me that a DLL file is already in use or in ROM, and then asks if it should try again. What is happening?

A. The Installation routine is set up to copy all the files needed to your HPC. This includes a DLL file called MFCCE20.DLL. Some HPCs (mainly HP models) already have this file in ROM - this saves RAM. When the installation routine asks you if it should try again, simply say no and it will skip over the file. Pocket Universe will install and run perfectly.

Q. Does Pocket Universe run on Palmsize PCs?

A. A new version specifically designed for Palmsized PCs can be found here. IMPORTANT: Don't buy the HPC version and expect it to run on the Palmsize PC or vice versa!

Q. I have the Palmsize PC version. When I select Save, I get a message about an unexpected error.

A. This has been corrected on latest PsPC versions.

Q. Will it run under Windows CE v1?

A. No, and there is unlikely to be a version for this version of the OS. Sorry!

Q. Occasionally when I tap the screen to centre an object, it doesn't appear in the middle of the screen.

A. First of all, make sure that your stylus is calibrated properly. Use the stylus applet from the Control Panel. Sometimes when Pocket Universe is asked to centre the display at a point a long way from its current location, it will be ever-so-slightly wide of the mark. All you need to do is tap the item you want to centre a second time for a more accurate fix.

Q. When I try to set up the date, time and location for an eclipse the sun and moon don't seem to be totally overlapped as they should. Why is this?

A. In order to make Pocket Universe run as quickly as possible, some of the calculations involved are simplified slightly. Make sure you switch on the Parallax option from the Display Option as this will help. Sticky Software will try and improve the accuracy of the sun and moon positions, and will release a free update to all existing owners if and when it becomes available. In general use, the accuracy of Pocket Universe should prove more than adequate.

Q. After I enter my location, it doesn't stay when I restart Pocket Universe.

A. Make sure you select Save Settings from the File Menu to store your current settings. It's these which will be used every time Pocket Universe starts.

Q. I need a quick way of zooming in the out. The I and O keys don't do it quickly enough.

A. Remember that you can use the Shift key at the same time to increase the jump in magnification. However, the easiest and quickest way to zoom is to use the toolbox's Zoom buttons. Tap them to quickly select between four different settings.

Q. I've noticed that the Sun and Moon don't seem to be the right size in the display.

A. The Sun and Moon are drawn as disks of a fixed size, unless the Display Option Real Size is switched on.

Q. What is the TimeZone?

A. As a result of the Earth being spherical and rotating, the Sun appears to rise and set at different times depending on where you are. We could use one universal time, but then when it was sunrise in London at 9am, it could be the middle of the night in Sidney, Australia: but still 9am. For this reason the world is split into TimeZones. These are an offset from the "official" time, when is Greenwich Mean Time. GMT is the time at Greenwich in England: the location of 0 degrees Longitude.

Q. When I Goto the Sun or Moon, they aren't there!

A. Check to see if you have switched on the display option "Hide Low", as this causes all objects (other than constellation outlines) beneath the horizon not to be displayed.

Q. What is daylight saving time?

A. Depending on the season, the days seem to get longer or shorter. To make the most of available daylight, some countries shift the time one hour forward for the months of March to October (or October to March in the southern hemisphere). The exact dates will differ from country to country. Checking the Daylight Saving from the Set Time/Date dialog will cause Pocket Universe to assume that your clock is actually one hour ahead of what your local time should be.

Q. Redrawing the screen seems to take a long time. Can't you create a larger display and then scroll around it?

A. It's true that on some HPC's it can take a irritatingly long time to update the display. This is due to the number of calculations involved, and all the graphics operations required. To speed it up, make sure you are only displaying the objects you need to: switch off the horizon for example, and constellation outlines. It's not possible to construct the display in advance and scroll around it, because the view changes totally depending on where you are looking. Remember the sky appears to be on the inside of a large sphere, not flat on a map or plan. The best way to speed up the display is to use a fast HPC: the Philips Velo models are exceptionally fast, as are current the Compaq models.

Q. What are Jupiter's Moons?

A. The giant gas planet Jupiter has a host of small planetoids orbiting it. The four largest were discovered by the astronomer Galileo, one of the first people to ever point a telescope at the night sky. They are known as the Galilean Moons in his honour. You can see them yourself with a small telescope or even binoculars. They appear as four points of light close to the disk of Jupiter. Some people claim to be able to see the brightest with their naked eye!

Q. I want to bring my handheld outside, anything to be wary of?

A. Take care with all electrical equipment if there is a heavy dew. When bringing your computer back inside, be careful with condensation problems. When using you handheld outside, the backlight may alter your night vision for the worse - consider using a red torch instead, or a stylus with a built-in light, such as those from Panache Ltd.

Q. Will you add support for Satellite tracking to Pocket Universe?

A. We're considering it, but can't promise anything at this stage.

Q. Can Pocket Universe be connected to a telescope?

A. Currently not. However, we are working on adding features to allow control of the Meade LX200. We are hoping to test this soon, and will make the upgrade available free to all existing owners.
